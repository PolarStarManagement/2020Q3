Section 2 Price and Volatility

2.1 Price Moves

The figure below shows the percentage changes of the rolled price series of the universe of commodities we follow. Green and red bars represent increases and decreases in prices respectively. The vertical dashed line shows the mean absolute price (11.3%) change across all the commodities shown.

Figure 2.1: 2020 Q3 price changes expressed as percentages

An outlier on the positive side is Lumber which had had a tremendous run since the corona virus outbreak. Lumber shortages were reported at Home Depots all over the United States with people staying home and getting busy with home improvement projects. This increased demand was couples with a lack of supply due to social distancing and forced layoffs at sawmills.

2.2 Volatility

Here we use the standard definition of volatility as the standard deviation of daily returns. We make use of the rolled price series for each of the commodities to determine returns. The gradient plots below highlight the changes in volatility between Q1, Q2 and Q3 of 2020. We separate the plots across different commodity sectors.

2.2.1 Energies

As we have noticed in the media there has been heightened activity in the energies space in the second quarter which culminated in negative prices on the front end of the curve. The plot below highlights the changes in volatility between the first, second and third quarters. We can see that crude oil and derivatives have experienced heightened volatilities during Q2. During Q3 we have gone back to much more muted volatilities.

Figure 2.2: Energies volatility gradient plot

2.2.3 Grains and Oilseeds

Below we split the grains and oilseed between the world and China. It is interesting to note that the second quarter did not show heightened volatility in the grains and oilseeds space. Moreover, some commodities even showed decreased volatility. During the third quarter we have seen very similar volatilities compared to the the other quarters.

Figure 2.4: Grains and oilseeds without China volatility gradient plot

It is interesting to see that Chinese grains and oilseeds showed decreased volatility for all commodities shown except corn during the second quarter. These commodities have shown an increaes in volatility during the third quarter.

Figure 2.5: China Grains and oilseeds volatility gradient plot
